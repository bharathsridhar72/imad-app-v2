In Calculus II, students defined the integral b a f (x) dx over a finite interval [a, b]. The function f was assumed to be continuous, or at least bounded, otherwise the integral was not guaranteed to exist. Assuming an antiderivative of f could be found, b a f (x) dx always existed, and was a number. In this section, we investigate what happens when these conditions are not met.
